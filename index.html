About Me

I am a PhD student with a scholarship from the Office of the Civil Service Commision of Thailand (OCSC). I receieved my Masters degree at Durham University (2019) and my undergraduate degree at Khon Kaen University (2014-2018). During my studies, I am interested in understanding how galaxies are formed and evolve throughout the cosmic time using supercomputers and galaxy survers. I am also enthusiastic about making scientific research more accesible to the general public through public outreach/activities.

Apart from stars/galaxies/Universe, I also enjoy playing football (a goal keeper for Ustinov College Men's Football team), painting&drawing, photography, and lifting weights!

Research

The Galaxy Luminonsity Function up to z ~ 1.3

This is an on-going project. We measure the luminosity function using a photometric redshift survey called the Physics of the Accelerating Universe Survey (PAUS) combined with a galaxy mock catalogue from a semi-analytical galaxy formation model GALFORM. PAUS provides a very precise measurement of galaxy redshifts. Galaxies in the PAUS are selected in i-band CFHTLens filter to be i_AB <= 23. With information we can obtain from the GALFORM mock catalogue, we can model k-correction for obtaining the galaxy redshift magnitudes. In this work, we use the Random Forest Regression technique for predicting the absotlute rest-frame magnitudes of galaxies from their observed magnitudes. We apply the same selection function from PAUS to the mock catalogue, this allows us to understand the effects of the survey selection on the galaxy luminosity function. We find that the mock catalogue and the PAUS luminosity functions agree very well upto redshift z ~ 1.3. Howevery, the bright end of the luminosity function are shallower in the observation than that in the mock catalogue. This can be explained by the fact that the number of outliers in the photometric redshift measurements from PAUS is large in redshift bin z > 1.3.
